Waste Segregation Guide

Proper segregation is the first step towards a cleaner city. Here's a guide to help you sort your waste correctly.

compost

Organic (Wet Waste)

Any waste that comes from a plant or animal and is biodegradable.

What goes in:

  • Fruit & Vegetable Peels
  • Leftover Food
  • Egg Shells & Tea Bags
  • Garden Waste (leaves, twigs)

What STAYS OUT:

  • Plastics
  • Packaging
  • Batteries
recycling

Recyclable (Dry Waste)

Items that can be reprocessed into new products. Should be clean and dry.

What goes in:

  • Plastic Bottles & Containers
  • Paper & Cardboard
  • Glass Bottles & Jars
  • Metal Cans (tin, aluminum)

What STAYS OUT:

  • Food Waste
  • Styrofoam
  • Medical Waste
dangerous

Hazardous Waste

Waste that is potentially harmful to people or the environment. NEVER mix with other waste.

What goes in:

  • Batteries & Electronics
  • Paints & Solvents
  • Light Bulbs (CFL, tubes)
  • Expired Medicines

What STAYS OUT:

  • Everything else!
